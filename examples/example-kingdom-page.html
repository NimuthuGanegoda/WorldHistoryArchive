Anuradhapura Kingdom

437 BCE ‚Äì 1017 CE

The Anuradhapura Kingdom was the first major kingdom of Sri Lanka and served as the island's capital for over 1,400 years. It was the cradle of Sinhalese Buddhist civilization and became one of the most important centers of Buddhism in the ancient world.

The kingdom was renowned for its sophisticated irrigation systems, including massive reservoirs (tanks) and canal networks that supported agriculture across the dry zone. It was also home to magnificent stupas, monasteries, and the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree, which still stands today as the oldest historically authenticated tree in the world.

Key Features & Achievements

  • Irrigation Systems: Advanced network of tanks (reservoirs) and canals, including the Abhaya Wewa and Tissa Wewa
  • Religious Architecture: Massive stupas like Ruwanwelisaya, Jetavanaramaya, and Abhayagiri Dagoba
  • Sri Maha Bodhi: Sacred fig tree planted in 288 BCE, the oldest living tree with a documented history
  • Monastic Universities: Mahavihara, Abhayagiri, and Jetavana monasteries became major centers of Buddhist learning
