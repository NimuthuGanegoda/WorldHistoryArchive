Historical Sites & Monuments

Ancient architectural wonders and UNESCO World Heritage Sites of Sri Lanka

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Sigiriya Rock Fortress

5th century CE

Ancient rock fortress built by King Kashyapa I with famous frescoes

Sacred City of Anuradhapura

3rd century BCE - 10th century CE

Ancient capital with massive stupas and the sacred Bodhi tree

Ancient City of Polonnaruwa

11th - 13th century CE

Medieval capital with exquisite rock carvings and irrigation systems

Temple of the Tooth

17th-18th century CE

Sacred temple in Kandy housing the tooth relic of Buddha

Dambulla Cave Temple

1st century BCE

Largest cave temple complex with over 150 Buddha statues

Major Stupas

Ruwanwelisaya

Built: 140 BCE

Masterpiece by King Dutugemunu, one of the tallest ancient monuments

Jetavanaramaya

Built: 273-301 CE

Once the tallest stupa in the world at 122 meters

Abhayagiri Dagoba

Built: 89-77 BCE

Major monastery complex and center of Buddhist learning

Thuparamaya

Built: 247 BCE

First Buddhist stupa in Sri Lanka, built by King Devanampiya Tissa

Rock Temples & Fortresses

Gal Vihara

12th century CE

Four magnificent Buddha statues carved from a single granite wall

Yapahuwa Rock Fortress

13th century CE

Rock fortress with ornate staircase that housed the Tooth Relic

Avukana Buddha

5th century CE

40-foot standing Buddha carved from a single granite rock
